Start your own organic garden with these practical tips

Start with Easy Crops

Begin with herbs like basil, mint, and coriander. They're forgiving and grow quickly, giving you confidence to expand to vegetables.

Make Your Own Compost

Collect kitchen scraps (vegetable peels, coffee grounds) and dry leaves. Layer them in a bin and turn regularly for rich, free fertilizer.

Natural Pest Control

Use neem oil spray, garlic-pepper solution, or introduce beneficial insects like ladybugs. Avoid chemical pesticides completely.

Water Wisely

Water early morning or evening to reduce evaporation. Use mulch around plants to retain moisture and suppress weeds naturally.

Companion Planting

Plant marigolds with tomatoes to repel pests, or grow basil near peppers to enhance flavor and growth naturally.

Soil Health First

Test your soil pH and add organic matter regularly. Healthy soil means healthy plants and better resistance to diseases.
